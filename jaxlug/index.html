[ WHAT IS INSTALLFEST? — README.MD ]

Your Linux Journey Starts Here

InstallFest is JAXLUG's annual hands-on event where our experienced members sit down with you — one-on-one — and help you install Linux on your personal computer. Whether you've never heard of Linux or you've been curious for years but never took the plunge, this is your day.

We support beginners, curious Windows users, Mac users who want more freedom, and anyone who just wants to try something new. Our volunteers are patient, friendly, and genuinely love helping people.

What We'll Do

  • Back up your existing data safely
  • Help you choose the right distro for your needs
  • Create a bootable USB drive
  • Install Linux (dual-boot or full install)
  • Configure drivers, WiFi, and basics
  • Answer all your questions — no judgment

Who Should Come?

  • Windows users tired of updates and bloat
  • Students wanting a developer-friendly OS
  • Anyone with an old PC slowing down
  • Privacy-conscious users
  • Gamers curious about Steam on Linux
  • Complete beginners — seriously, anyone

Bring With You

  • Your laptop or desktop (in a bag)
  • Power cable / charger
  • An external drive for backup (recommended)
  • Your Wi-Fi password if applicable
  • Your curiosity and patience
⚠ NOTE:
We will make every effort to preserve your data, but we strongly recommend backing up beforehand. JAXLUG is not responsible for data loss.

[ ABOUT JAXLUG — MAN PAGE ]

NAME

JAXLUG — Jacksonville Linux User's Group

SYNOPSIS

jaxlug [--meet monthly] [--learn] [--help] [--share] [--enjoy]

DESCRIPTION

JAXLUG is a volunteer-run user group founded in 1999 dedicated to promoting, supporting, and spreading Linux and free/open-source software in Northeast Florida.

We welcome everyone from curious beginners to grizzled sysadmins. Membership is free. Knowledge is freely shared.

HISTORY

Founded in 1999 during the first wave of Linux desktop adoption, JAXLUG has survived the browser wars, the rise and fall of Netscape, the systemd debates, and the Year of the Linux Desktop (any year now).

We've hosted install fests, LAN parties, kernel compile contests, and countless productive arguments about text editors.

OPTIONS

  • --attend-meetings
    Monthly meetings, first Thursday of each month
  • --mailing-list
    Join the discussion at jaxlug@groups.io
  • --irc / --matrix
    #jaxlug on Libera.Chat
  • --volunteer
    Help at events like InstallFest
  • --present
    Give a talk at a monthly meeting

CODE OF CONDUCT

Be excellent to each other. No gatekeeping. No platform shaming. Yes, we know you use Arch.

SEE ALSO

meetings(1), installfest(8), linux(1), freedom(7)

AUTHORS
